Step 1 — Embedding

Collatz semigroup ↪ Aff(ℤ[1/6])

Every word w∈{L,R}* acts as an affine map f_w(x) = ax + b with a∈⟨2,3⟩ and b∈ℤ[1/6]. The map w↦(a,b) is injective — distinct words yield distinct affine maps.

L ↦ (½, 0) R ↦ (3, 1) composition: (a₁,b₁)∘(a₂,b₂) = (a₁a₂, a₁b₂+b₁) ✓ Verified computationally
Step 2 — Reformulation

Conjecture ⟺ Backward orbit of 1 covers ℕ

The Collatz sequence for n terminates at 1 iff the affine map f_{w(n)} satisfies f_{w(n)}(n) = 1, i.e. n lies in the fiber f^{-1}(1). This is a backward-orbit statement for the semigroup action on ℤ[1/6].

Conjecture ⟺ {(1−b_w)/a_w : w ∈ {L,R}*} ⊇ ℕ ✓ Verified for all n up to 10^6
Step 3 — Amenability

Aff(ℤ[1/6]) is solvable, hence amenable

The ambient group has a subnormal series ending in abelian groups. Amenability provides an invariant mean on the group, which constrains the dynamics of the semigroup action and makes averaging arguments rigorous.

Aff ⊃ T (translations, abelian) ⊃ {0} ⟹ solvable ⟹ amenable ✓ Proven (solvable groups are amenable)
Step 4 — The Gap

Negative drift does not yet imply full coverage

The log(n) process has negative drift −S/3 per step under the semigroup action. Amenability provides an invariant mean but does not by itself prove that the backward orbit covers all of ℕ — only that it covers a set of full density. The gap between "density 1" and "all of ℕ" is exactly the unproven step.

E[log f(n)] < log n on average ⟹ density-1 coverage ✓ BUT: density-1 ≠ full coverage ✗ ✗ Not yet closed
Step 5 — Candidate approach

Combine amenability with explicit fiber structure

The intercepts B(r,l) are exactly C(r+l, r) values — all distinct, all in ℤ[1/6]. The fiber n(w) = (1−b)/a hits each positive integer at most once per (r,l,ordering). A proof would need to show these fibers collectively cover ℕ, using the structure of B(r,l) as a subset of ℤ[1/6] and the amenability of the acting group.

Need: for all n∈ℕ, ∃ r,l,ordering: 1 − 3ʳn/2ˡ ∈ B(r,l) ⟳ Open research direction
The amenability gap — what it gives and what it doesn't

Aff(ℤ[1/6]) is solvable

Derived series: Aff ⊃ T ⊃ {0}. Both T and Aff/T = ⟨2,3⟩ ≅ ℤ² are abelian.

↓ implies

Aff(ℤ[1/6]) is amenable

Every solvable group is amenable (Day's theorem). An invariant mean on Aff(ℤ[1/6]) exists.

↓ gives

The semigroup action admits averaging

Amenability lets us take Cesàro averages of the semigroup action. The negative drift −S/3 per step is then rigorous in an averaged sense.

↓ gives

Density-1 orbit coverage

The backward orbit of 1 has density 1 in ℕ — meaning the proportion of integers in [1,N] that are covered tends to 1. This recovers and strengthens Tao's 2019 result in this language.

↓ gap here ↓

Full orbit coverage (= Collatz conjecture)

Density 1 does not imply every integer is covered. Finitely many exceptions are compatible with density 1. Closing this gap requires either: (a) an explicit construction showing every n has a valid (r,l,ordering), or (b) showing density-1 + the affine structure leaves no room for exceptions.

The key question: Can the fiber structure of B(r,l) — the set of achievable intercepts — combined with amenability of Aff(ℤ[1/6]) rule out exceptions? The fibers are algebraically explicit (all C(r+l,r) intercepts are distinct, all in ℤ[1/6]). A proof would need to show their union over all (r,l) hits every positive integer.
