Key finding: Every word in {L, R, L⁻¹, R⁻¹} acts as an affine map f(x) = ax + b over ℤ[1/6]. Two words are equal iff they have the same slope and intercept. The semigroup is therefore isomorphic to a sub-semigroup of Aff(ℤ[1/6]), not a free semigroup. The word problem is decidable — just compute (a,b) and compare.
Relations are checked symbolically via the affine map (a,b). A relation w = identity holds iff a=1, b=0. A relation w₁ = w₂ holds iff both words produce the same (a,b).
Words with the same (a,b) are equal in the semigroup. The number of distinct words of length n with r R's and l L's equals the number of distinct intercepts, which equals C(r+l, r) — the full binomial coefficient. So no two distinct orderings of the same (r,l) letters give the same affine map.
