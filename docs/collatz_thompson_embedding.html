Key Finding
The Collatz semigroup is not a subgroup of G₍₂,₃₎ over ℕ, because R⁻¹ is not always defined. It is a sub-semigroup of G₍₂,₃₎ over ℤ[1/6].
The embedding over ℤ[1/6] extends the domain. Orbits that reach n=1 in ℕ correspond to orbits that reach the fixed point x=1 in ℤ[1/6].

Collatz Inverse Tree (backward orbits from root n=1)
The inverse Collatz tree rooted at n=1. Each node has left child 2n (L⁻¹) and right child (n−1)/3 when n≡1 mod 3 and (n−1)/3 is odd (R⁻¹). This is a binary tree, placing the Collatz problem naturally in the domain of Thompson's group F and its (2,3) generalisation.
